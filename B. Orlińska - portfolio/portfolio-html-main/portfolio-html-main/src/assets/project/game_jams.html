ORDO (2025)

Game Jam Górniczo-Hutniczy

ORDO is a game made in 48 hours in Unity for the Game Jam Górniczo-Hutniczy at AGH Kraków, Poland.

The game was awarded 3rd place.

Humanity destroyed Earth—wars, greed, and disasters pushed the planet to its limit. In desperation, ORDO, an advanced AI, was created to save the world. ORDO found a solution: it neutralized the greatest threat—humans—not by killing, but by trapping them in simulations to reshape their morality. You, deemed highly dangerous, have three chances to prove you can be saved.

Game available at: https://viwien.itch.io/ordo

My responsibilites in the project included:

  • Gathering references for the levels
  • Creating block-outs that support storytelling
  • Designing gameplay mechanics
  • Making lists of necessary items for 2D and 3D artists
  • Editing cutscenes

Levels created for the game

For the game I've designed 3 levels:

  • The Playground
  • The Forest
  • The Supermarket

one of them supports interactive storytelling that reacts to player's actions.

The game's purpose is to make the player stop doing evil - the palcement of objects on the levels makes it very easy to do what we're being asked not to do.

HellHamster: Symphony of Destruction (2025)

Łódzki Game Jam 9 (Global Game Jam)

HellHamster was created during a 48 hour Łódzki Game Jam by a team of 4 people using Unity.

You've joined a Norwegian death metal band, only to be tricked into an occult ritual—turning you into a hamster!
Now trapped in a deadly candyland with gun-toting unicorns, you must navigate a chaotic fortress, switching between agility (bubble mode) and firepower (no bubble mode). Find the magical sphere to regain your human form!

Game available at: https://pitahaya-games.itch.io/hellhamster

My responsibilites in the project included:

  • Gathering references for the level
  • Creating a block-out of the level
  • Making lists of necessary items for the 3D artist
  • Editing cutscenes

Creating HellHamster was a great practice of designing non-linear levels. There are 3
independent paths you can follow in order to complete the game

Kandydat na bladawca (2024)

Lem Jam (Cyberiada)

Kandydat na bladawca is a game that was created in 24 hours in Unity by 10 people for the 2025 LemJam, part of the nationwide Cyberiada game development contest.

The game was awarded 57/80 points by the Cyberiada jury and was broadcast on the "Gramy na Maxa" channel.

While inspired by Lem’s work, the game introduces a cognitive dissonance that misleads players about its rules. You must evaluate candidates based on four criteria—but should you follow the instructions literally or trust your instincts? In this game, your sight might be your greatest enemy.

Game available at: https://cyber-sea.itch.io/kandydat-na-bladawca

My responsibilites in the project included:

  • Creating a block-out of the playable space
  • Making lists of necessary items for the 3D artist
  • Editing cutscenes

Creating this game was really educative. It was my first time
using Unity and it was my first time designing a level that was a UI hub at the same time.

Someone Will Lose (Everything) (2024)

1-BIT Jam

The game was created in Godot in a week for 1-bit Game Jam by a team of 5 people - for most of us it was our first game jam experience that we additionally had to reconcile with school work.

The game was 38th out of over 300 games.

Tekla had it all—popularity, talent, and love. No one expected her suicide.
Interrogate her loved ones, uncover their secrets, and solve the mystery. - by playing 1-level demo of Someone Will Lose (Everything).

Game available at: https://schildenfeld.itch.io/someone-will-lose-everything

My responsibilites in the project included:

  • Creating layouts of 2 levels
  • Implementing the level in Godot
  • Making lists of necessary items for the 2D artist

Due to a lot of school responsibilities we were only able to implement 1 out of 3 planned levels.
I have designed 2 out of 3 while testing my creativity levels by trying to make the layouts reuse as many assets as possible.
